- Momentum, Energy, and Mass
A beam of trapped light, that does not change its position in space, can be described by its electromagnetic momentum density per length unit function \(\overline p_0(\overline x, t) \), so the electromagnetic energy density is \(e_0(\overline x, t)=C\lvert\overline p_0\rvert\).
We can integrate along the path of light to get the total momentum \(\overline P_0=\int\overline p_0dl=0\) (This is zero momentum of a particle at rest)
The total energy is \(E_0=\int C\lvert\overline p_0\rvert dl\ne 0\) and we should note that, for beams of light, the value of the energy is redundant, as it does not add any information, once with know the momentum.
To put the particle in motion, we add momentum \(\overline P_M\). This momentum spreads along the beam as a constant addition of momentum density \(\overline p_M=\frac{\overline P_M}{L}\), where \(L=\int dl\). We can define the total momentum density (\(\overline p_T\)) as the sum of the “rest momentum density” (\(\overline p_0\)), and the “motion momentum density” (\(\overline p_M\)).
So now the total energy is \(E_T=\int C\lvert\overline p_0+\overline p_M\rvert dl\) and we get the following \[E_T^2=(\int C\lvert\overline p_T\rvert dl)^2=(\int C\lvert\overline p_0+\overline p_M\rvert dl)^2=\] \[=C^2\int\int (\overline p_0+\overline p_M)^2dl^2= C^2\int_{0}^{L}\int_{0}^{L}(\overline p_0^2+2\overline p_0\cdot\overline p_M+\overline p_M^2)dl^2=\] \[=(\int C\lvert\overline p_0\rvert dl)^2+2C^2L\overline p_M\int_{0}^{L}\overline p_0dl+L^2\overline p_M^2C^2=\] \[=E_0^2+2C^2\overline P_M\overline P_0+\overline P_M^2C^2=\quad (\textrm{remember that } \overline P_0=0)\] \[\textrm{so we finally get }\quad E_T^2=E_0^2+\overline P_M^2C^2\] \[\textrm{also known as }\quad \boxed{E^2=M^2C^4+P^2C^2}\]
