- Ether Flow Around a Torus
We are looking for steady state ether flow around the torus. The element that creates the flow is a ring of radius \(a\). This means that the whole situation has a toroidal symmetry.
We will assume that this is a potential flow, and look for the potential \(\phi(x, y, z)\). This will be the potential for the ether flow \(\rho\overline{\nu}=\triangledown\phi\). Due to the toroidal symmetry, we solve the equation using toroidal coordinates,
Note that our solutions for \(\phi\) describe a flow around the torus, so \(\phi\) is has some discontinuity as we go around the torus. However, the flow \(\rho\overline{\nu}=\triangledown\phi\) is continuous and also rotation free outside the torus, i.e. \(\triangledown\times\rho\overline{\nu}=0\).
From Moon & Spencer [5] we take the Toroidal coordinates \[0\leq\eta<+\infty,\quad-\pi<\theta\leq+\pi,\quad0\leq\psi<2\pi\] \[x=\frac{a\sinh\eta\cos\psi}{\cosh\eta-\cos\theta}\] \[y=\frac{a\sinh\eta\sin\psi}{\cosh\eta-\cos\theta}\] \[z=\frac{a\sin\theta}{\cosh\eta-\cos\theta}\]
The inverse coordinates calculation is \[\eta=\frac{1}{2}\ln\frac{\left(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}+a\right)^2+z^2}{\left(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}-a\right)^2+z^2}\] \[\theta=\arcsin\frac{\left(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}+a\right)^2+z^2+\left(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}-a\right)^2+z^2-4a^2} {2\sqrt{\left(\left(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}+a\right)^2+z^2\right)\left(\left(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}-a\right)^2+z^2\right)}}= \arcsin\frac{x^2+y^2+z^2-a^2} {\sqrt{\left(\left(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}+a\right)^2+z^2\right)\left(\left(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}-a\right)^2+z^2\right)}} \] \[\psi=\arctan\frac{y}{x}\]

Solving the Laplace equation
In these coordinates the laplacian becomes \[\triangledown^2\phi=\frac{(\cosh\eta-\cos\theta)^3}{a^2\sinh\eta}\left\{\frac{\partial}{\partial\eta} \left(\frac{\sinh\eta}{\cosh\eta-\cos\theta}\right)+ \sinh\eta\frac{\partial}{\partial\theta}\left(\frac{1}{\cosh\eta-\cos\theta}\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial\theta}\right)\right\}+ \frac{(\cosh\eta-\cos\theta)^2}{a^2\sinh^2\eta}\frac{\partial^2\phi}{\partial\psi^2}\] In order to solve the equation \(\triangledown^2\phi=0\) we set \[\phi=(\cosh\eta-\cos\theta)^{\frac{1}{2}}H(\eta)\Theta(\theta)\Psi(\psi)\] So the equation can be separated into three equations, and we get \[\frac{\partial^2H}{\partial\eta^2}+\coth\eta\frac{\partial H}{\partial\eta}+\left(\frac{1}{4}-\alpha_2-\frac{\alpha_3}{sinh^2\eta}\right)H=0\] \[\frac{\partial^2\Theta}{\partial\theta^2}+\alpha_2\Theta=0\] \[\frac{\partial^2\Psi}{\partial\psi^2}+\alpha_3\Psi=0\]
The equations for \(\Theta\) and \(\Psi\) lead us to define \[\alpha_2=p^2\quad\alpha_3=q^2\] with \(p\) and \(q\) as integer numbers, so we end with the following solutions \[\frac{\partial^2\Theta}{\partial\theta^2}+p^2\Theta=0\quad\Rightarrow\quad\Theta=A_2\sin p\theta+B_2\cos p\theta\] \[\frac{\partial^2\Psi}{\partial\psi^2}+q^2\Psi=0\quad\Rightarrow\quad\Psi=A_3\sin p\psi+B_3\cos p\psi\] As for the \(\eta\), we define \(\xi=\cosh\eta\) then we get \[\frac{\partial H}{\partial\eta}=\frac{\partial H}{\partial\xi}\frac{\partial\xi}{\partial\eta}=\frac{\partial H}{\partial\xi}\sinh\eta\] \[\frac{\partial^2H}{\partial\eta^2}=\frac{\partial}{\partial\eta}\left(\frac{\partial H}{\partial\xi}\frac{\partial\xi}{\partial\eta}\right)= \frac{\partial^2H}{\partial\xi^2}\sinh^2\eta+\frac{\partial H}{\partial\xi}\cosh\eta\] we can change the equation for \(\eta\) into and equation for \(\xi\) as \[\left(\xi^2-1\right)\frac{d^2H}{d\xi^2}+2\xi\frac{dH}{d\xi}-\left[\frac{q^2}{\xi^2-1}+\left(p^2-\frac{1}{4}\right)\right] H=0\] with the general solution \[H=A\mathcal{P}^q_{p-\frac{1}{2}}(\cosh\eta)+B\mathcal{Q}^q_{p-\frac{1}{2}}(\cosh\eta)\] where \[P^{q}_{p-\frac{1}{2}}\left(\cosh\eta\right)=\frac{\Gamma\left(\frac{1}{2}-q\right)}{\pi^{1/2}\left(1-e^{-2\eta}\right)^{q}e^{(p+(1/2))\eta}}\mathbf{F}\left(\tfrac{1}{2}-q,\tfrac{1}{2}+p-q;1-2q;1-e^{-2\eta}\right)\] \[\boldsymbol{Q}^{q}_{p-\frac{1}{2}}\left(\cosh\eta\right)=\frac{\pi^{1/2}\left(1-e^{-2\eta}\right)^{q}}{e^{(p+(1/2))\eta}}\mathbf{F}\left(q+\tfrac{1}{2},p+q+\tfrac{1}{2};p+1;e^{-2\eta}\right)\]
We will examine first the solution in the case \(p=q=0\) where we get \[\left(\xi^2-1\right)\frac{d^2H}{d\xi^2}+2\xi\frac{dH}{d\xi}+\frac{1}{4}H=0\] with the general solution How to solve?
How do we solve an equation like \[\left(\xi^2-1\right)\frac{d^2H}{d\xi^2}+2\xi\frac{dH}{d\xi}+\frac{1}{4}H=0\]
Solutions of a first kind
We first start with solutions of a first kind. It has the following form \[H(\xi)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^n\quad\Rightarrow\quad\frac{dH}{d\xi}= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n a_n\xi^{n-1}\quad\Rightarrow\quad\frac{d^2H}{d\xi^2}=\sum_{n=2}^{\infty}n(n-1)a_n\xi^{n-2} \] Put into the equation \[\left(\xi^2-1\right)\sum_{n=2}^{\infty}n(n-1)a_n\xi^{n-2}+2\xi\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n a_n\xi^{n-1}+\frac{1}{4}\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^n=0\] \[-\sum_{n=2}^{\infty}n(n-1)a_n\xi^{n-2}+\sum_{n=2}^{\infty}n(n-1)a_n\xi^n +2\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n a_n\xi^n+\frac{1}{4}\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^n=0\] \[-\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(n+2)(n+1)a_{n+2}\xi^n+\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}n(n-1)a_n\xi^n +2\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}n a_n\xi^n+\frac{1}{4}\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^n=0\] \[\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\xi^n\left[-(n+2)(n+1)a_{n+2}+n(n-1)a_n+2n a_n+\frac{1}{4}a_n\right]=0\] So, for every \(n\) we get the relation \[-(n+2)(n+1)a_{n+2}+n(n-1)a_n+2n a_n+\frac{1}{4}a_n=0\] \[a_{n+2}=\frac{n(n+1)+\frac{1}{4}}{(n+2)(n+1)}a_n\] \[a_{n+2}=\frac{(n+\frac{1}{2})(n+\frac{1}{2})}{(n+2)(n+1)}a_n\] Setting \(a_0=A, a_1=B\) and defining\[(x)_{n,d}\equiv(1)(x)(x+d)(x+2d)...(x+(n-1)d)\quad(x)_n=(x)_{n,1}\] We can write the solution \[H(\xi)=A\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(1/2)_{n,2}(1/2)_{n,2}}{(2n)!}\xi^{2n}+B\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(3/2)_{n,2}(3/2)_{n,2}}{(2n+1)!}\xi^{2n+1}\] We use the identities \[(x)_{n,2}=2^n(x/2)_n\quad(2n)!=(1)_{n,2}(2)_{n,2}\quad(2n+1)!=(2)_{n,2}(3)_{n,2}\] To get \[H(\xi)=A\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(1/4)_n(1/4)_n}{(1/2)_n(1)_n}\xi^{2n}+B\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(3/4)_n(3/4)_n}{(1)_n(3/2)_n}\xi^{2n+1}\] This can be written as \[H(\xi)=A\cdot{}_2F_1\left(\frac{1}{4}, \frac{1}{4}; \frac{1}{2}; \xi^2\right) + B\xi\cdot{}_2F_1\left(\frac{3}{4}, \frac{3}{4}; \frac{3}{2};\xi^2\right)\]
Solutions of a second kind
We will be more interested with solutions of a second kind. We look at a more general equation \[\left(\xi^2-1\right)\frac{d^2H}{d\xi^2}+2\xi\frac{dH}{d\xi}-\nu(\nu+1)H=0\] Solutions have the following form \[H(\xi)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^{-\nu-n}\quad\Rightarrow\quad\frac{dH}{d\xi}= \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-\nu-n) a_n\xi^{-\nu-n-1}\quad\Rightarrow\quad\frac{d^2H}{d\xi^2}=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-\nu-n)(-\nu-n-1)a_n\xi^{-\nu-n-2} \] Put into the equation \[\left(\xi^2-1\right)\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-\nu-n)(-\nu-n-1)a_n\xi^{-\nu-n-2}+2\xi\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-\nu-n) a_n\xi^{-\nu-n-1}-\nu(\nu+1)\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^{-\nu-n}=0\] \[-\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-\nu-n)(-\nu-n-1)a_n\xi^{-\nu-n-2}+\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-\nu-n)(-\nu-n-1)a_n\xi^{-\nu-n} +2\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-\nu-n) a_n\xi^{-\nu-n}-\nu(\nu+1)\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^{-\nu-n}=0\] \[-\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(\nu+n)(\nu+n+1)a_n\xi^{-\nu-n-2}+\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^{-\nu-n}\left[(\nu+n)(\nu+n+1) -2(\nu+n)-\nu(\nu+1)\right]=0\] \[-\sum_{n=2}^{\infty}(\nu+n-2)(\nu+n-1)a_{n-2}\xi^{-\nu-n}+\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^{-\nu-n}\left[(\nu+n)(\nu+n-1)-\nu(\nu+1)\right]=0\] \[-\sum_{n=2}^{\infty}(\nu+n-2)(\nu+n-1)a_{n-2}\xi^{-\nu-n}+\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n\xi^{-\nu-n}\left[(n-1)(n+2\nu)\right]=0\] And this amounts to: \[For\quad n=0,1\quad a_n(n-1)(n+2\nu)=0\quad\Rightarrow\quad a_0=0,\quad a_1 can be anything \] \[For\quad n>2\quad (\nu+n-2)(\nu+n-1)a_{n-2}=a_n(n-1)(n+2\nu)\quad\Rightarrow\quad a_n=a_{n-2}\frac{(\nu-2+n)(\nu-1+n)}{(-1+n)(2\nu+n)}\quad\Rightarrow\quad a_{n+2}=a_n\frac{(\nu+n)(\nu+1+n)}{(1+n)(2\nu+2+n)} \] We can write the solution \[H(\xi)=B\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(\nu+1)_{n,2}(\nu+2)_{n,2}}{\left(2\nu+3\right)_{n,2}(2)_{n,2}}\xi^{-\nu-(2n+1)}\] To get \[H(\xi)=B\xi^{-\nu-1}\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(\frac{\nu}{2}+\frac{1}{2})_n(\frac{\nu}{2}+1)_n}{\left(\nu+\frac{3}{2}\right)_nn!}\xi^{-2n}\] This can be written as \[H(\xi)=B\xi^{-\nu-1}\cdot{}_2F_1\left(\frac{\nu}{2}+\frac{1}{2}, \frac{\nu}{2}+1; \nu+\frac{3}{2};\xi^{-2}\right)\] In out case \(\nu=-\frac{1}{2}\) which gives \(\nu(\nu+1)=-\frac{1}{4}\) so the function becomes \[H(\xi)=B\xi^{-\frac{1}{2}}\cdot{}_2F_1\left(\frac{1}{4}, \frac{3}{4}; 1;\xi^{-2}\right)\]
\[H=A\mathcal{P}_{-\frac{1}{2}}(\cosh\eta)+B\mathcal{Q}_{-\frac{1}{2}}(\cosh\eta)\] \[\mathcal{Q}_{-\frac{1}{2}}(\xi)=B\xi^{-\frac{1}{2}}\cdot{}_2F_1\left(\frac{1}{4}, \frac{3}{4}; 1;\xi^{-2}\right)\] \[P_{-\frac{1}{2}}\left(\cosh\eta\right)=\frac{\Gamma\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)}{\pi^{1/2}e^{(1/2)\eta}}\mathbf{F}\left(\tfrac{1}{2},\tfrac{1}{2};1;1-e^{-2\eta}\right)\] \[\boldsymbol{Q}_{-\frac{1}{2}}\left(\cosh\eta\right)=\frac{\pi^{1/2}}{e^{(1/2)\eta}}\mathbf{F}\left(\tfrac{1}{2},\tfrac{1}{2};1;e^{-2\eta}\right)\] \[\frac{\partial^2\Theta}{\partial\theta^2}=0\quad\Rightarrow\quad\Theta=A_2+B_2\theta\] \[\frac{\partial^2\Psi}{\partial\psi^2}=0\quad\Rightarrow\quad\Psi=A_3+B_3\psi\]
We recall that the total potential has the form of \[\phi=(\cosh\eta-\cos\theta)^{\frac{1}{2}}H(\eta)\Theta(\theta)\Psi(\psi)\]
