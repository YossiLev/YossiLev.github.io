Introduction

The original Maxwell's equations are often seen as complex and unintuitive.

Compare with the equations that we currently teach in school. More modern formulations that are more compact and elegant.

$$\nabla \cdot \vec{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}$$ $$\nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0$$ $$\nabla \times \vec{E} = - \frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t}$$ $$\nabla \times \vec{B} = \mu_0 \vec{J} + \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial \vec{E}}{\partial t}$$

and some more.

$$\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot \vec{J} = 0$$ $$\vec{B} = \nabla \times \vec{A} \qquad \vec{E} = -\nabla \phi - \frac{\partial \vec{A}}{\partial t}$$

Space Algebra

Add multiplication to vector space

We start with 3 base vectors $$e_1, e_2, e_3$$ They represent the three dimensions of space. We can then define a new type of multiplication, called the geometric product, that allows us to combine these vectors in a way that captures both their magnitude and direction.
