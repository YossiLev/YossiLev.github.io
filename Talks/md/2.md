2. **Define matching conditions for free-space E,B**  
    Treat the region inside the torus as the electromagnetic source (compute $\rho_\text{eff}=\nabla\!\cdot(H\mathbf{L})$, $\mathbf{J}_\text{eff}=\nabla\times(H\boldsymbol{\omega})-\partial_t(H\mathbf{L})$). Solve Maxwell‚Äôs equations outside with these compact sources. The boundary values

$$\mathbf{E}_\text{outside}\cdot\hat{n}=\mathbf{E}_H\cdot\hat{n},\qquad  
\mathbf{B}_\text{outside}\times\hat{n}=\mathbf{B}_H\times\hat{n}$$

will then be continuous (or differ only by thin-surface currents if you choose discontinuous tails). Since the sign of $\mathbf{E}_H$ at the boundary is locked to the sign of $H$ via the relation above, the outside field inherits the outward/inward orientation.

So the core idea: keep the _sign_ of the product $v_\phi\omega_\theta$ fixed all the way to the boundary (only the magnitude decays). That guarantees an $\mathbf{L}$ on the surface that points the desired way.

# 4 ‚Äî A concrete smoothly varying example (profile)

To be explicit, pick smooth radial profiles inside the tube:

$$v_\phi(r)=V_0\cos^2\!\big(\tfrac{\pi r}{2a}\big), \qquad 0\le r\le a,$$

so $v_\phi(0)=V_0,\; v_\phi(a)=0$. Then

$$\omega_\theta(r) \approx \frac{1}{r}\frac{d}{dr}\big(r v_\phi(r)\big)$$

is easily computed and has the same sign as $V_0$ (except possibly tiny sign-change near $r=0$ if you choose very pathological shapes ‚Äî avoid those). Thus $H(r)=v_\phi\omega_\theta$ and $L_r$ keep the sign of $V_0$. Flip $V_0\to -V_0$ to get the mirror particle.

Use a similar smooth sign-preserving tail for helicity $H(r)=H_0 f(r)$ with $f(0)=1$, $f(a)=\varepsilon\ll1$, so inside the torus $H$ is large and outside $H\to 0$. The important thing is _sign preservation_, not magnitude.

# 5 ‚Äî Ensuring outside fields behave like Coulomb / EM

With the boundary Lamb orientation fixed, the rest is standard:

* compute effective $\rho,\mathbf{J}$ inside from $H\mathbf{L},H\boldsymbol{\omega}$;
* solve Maxwell in free space (Green‚Äôs functions) ‚Äî far field will be Coulomb/Biot‚ÄìSavart like and will keep the sign (incoming/outgoing) dictated by the boundary normal component of $\mathbf{E}_H$.

Thus you do **not** need to make $\mathbf{L}$ itself nonzero arbitrarily far outside ‚Äî you just need it to have the right sign at the boundary, and that sign is inherited by the outside Maxwell solution.
