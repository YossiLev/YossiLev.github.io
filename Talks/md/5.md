## What is a conformal transformation?

A **conformal transformation** is a mapping that **preserves angles locally**. That means:

* If two curves intersect at some point, the **angle between them is unchanged** after the transformation.
* Shapes may stretch or shrink, but **infinitesimal shapes keep their form (no shear)**.

Mathematically, in a space with metric $g_{\mu\nu}(x)$, a conformal transformation rescales the metric:

$$g_{\mu\nu}(x) \;\;\to\;\; \Omega^2(x)\, g_{\mu\nu}(x)$$

where $\Omega(x)$ is a **nonzero scalar function**.

So:

* Lengths change (because of $\Omega(x)$)
* Angles stay the same (because scaling cancels out in ratios)

---

## Intuition (geometric picture)

Think of drawing a tiny circle at each point:

* Under a general transformation → it can become an ellipse (angles distorted)
* Under a conformal transformation → it becomes **another circle (just scaled)**
