The Curvature of Gravitation
Apples fall to the ground, and planets orbit around the sun. Isaac Newton explained both findings, claiming that each body with mass exerts an attraction force on every other mass, causing the other mass to change its speed. The calculation of the attraction force between mass 1 and mass 2 is given by the formula . F , equals G Mass 1 times Mass 2, divided by square of the distance between the masses
\[F=\frac{GM_1M_2}{R^2}\]
After introducing the theory of relativity, Albert Einstein tried the generalize the theory to include relative accelerated motion. The result was a more general theory, to be later named "General Relativity". The theory gave a new explanation to attractive forces of gravity, and became the new gravity theory. It suggested that all the masses affect the "fabric of space-time" and bend/curve/distort it. In this space-time the motion of all the stuff (massive objects but also light!) is distorted as they follow the curved paths of the curved space-time. The new formula gives the connection between "Energy-Momentum T", which is the new name for Mass, and the geometric distortion of space time, now called "Einstein Curvature G". Both G and T are defined at each point of space, and at each moment of time. The formula is ever so simple, but the simple shape is misleading. \[G=T\] In reality the formula is so complicated that we could never provide a solution for it (except in some simple cases).
But still, we remain with all masses curve space-time, and then masses move in space-time along the curve paths that are the "straightest" curves available on the curved space-time. Only when there are not masses at all (\(T=0\)), do we get no distortion (\(G=0\)), and then we call it zero-curvature of flat-space.
